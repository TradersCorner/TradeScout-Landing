2025 Contractor Cost Guide by Service Type

Painting contractors cost $25-65 per hour or $1.50-6.00 per square foot. Interior painting averages $2-4/sq ft, exterior $3-6/sq ft. Primer and paint included. Wallpaper removal adds $1-3/sq ft. Cabinet painting $30-60 per linear foot. Deck staining $2-5/sq ft.

Roofing contractors charge $5-15 per square foot for replacement. Asphalt shingles $3-8/sq ft, metal roofing $7-14/sq ft, tile $8-18/sq ft. Roof repair $300-1500 typical. Gutter installation $4-9 per linear foot. Storm damage repairs vary widely.

Flooring contractors charge $3-15/sq ft installed. Carpet $2-8/sq ft, hardwood $6-12/sq ft, laminate $3-8/sq ft, tile $5-15/sq ft, luxury vinyl $4-10/sq ft. Subfloor repairs extra. Removal/disposal additional cost.

Plumbing contractors average $75-150 per hour. Toilet installation $200-500, faucet $150-400, water heater $1200-3000, sewer line $100-300/linear foot. Emergency calls premium rates. Permits typically required.

Electrical contractors charge $50-120 per hour. Outlet installation $100-200, panel upgrade $1200-3000, whole house rewiring $3000-8000, ceiling fan $150-500. All work requires permits and inspection.

How to Select the Right Contractor for Your Project

Research contractors thoroughly: verify license through state board, confirm insurance coverage, check Better Business Bureau rating, read online reviews, ask for recent references, visit recent job sites if possible. Get 3-5 written estimates comparing scope, materials, timeline, warranty terms.

Red flags to avoid: door-to-door solicitation, cash-only payment, large upfront payments, no written contract, no license/insurance, significantly low bids, high-pressure tactics, no local references, no physical business address.

Contract essentials: detailed scope of work, specific materials/brands, start and completion dates, payment schedule tied to milestones, change order procedures, cleanup responsibilities, warranty terms, lien waiver provisions.

When Do You Need Permits for Contractor Work?

Most electrical, plumbing, structural, HVAC, and roofing work requires permits. Kitchen and bathroom remodels typically need permits. Cosmetic work like painting usually doesn't. Requirements vary by location - check with local building department.

Contractors should handle permit applications and fees. Permits protect homeowners by ensuring code compliance and proper inspections. Work without required permits can cause insurance and resale issues.

Best Times to Hire Contractors by Season

Spring: Ideal for exterior work starting - painting, roofing, landscaping. Book early as contractors fill up quickly. Good weather allows projects to stay on schedule.

Summer: Peak season for exterior work. Higher demand may increase prices. Plan well in advance. Good for major projects with predictable weather.

Fall: Great time for interior projects. Contractors may offer discounts as exterior season ends. Perfect for planning winter projects.

Winter: Best prices for interior work. Limited exterior work availability. Storm damage repairs may take longer due to weather.

Safe Payment Practices When Hiring Contractors

Never pay large amounts upfront. Typical payment schedule: 10% down, progress payments tied to completion milestones, final payment after completion and satisfaction. Pay by check or credit card for protection.

For materials: pay suppliers directly or get lien waivers. Large projects may require materials deposit, but verify delivery before payment. Get receipts for all payments and maintain detailed records.
